## 1. PAGE STRATEGY

### Objective
Transform the AI Consult page from a single high-ticket offering to a two-tier service that captures both enterprise assessment clients and individual/small team coaching clients. The page now serves as a gateway addressing the full spectrum of AI maturity—from "I don't know where to start" to "We need a comprehensive AI strategy."

### Two Audiences

**Tier 1: Individual Contributors & Small Teams**
- Busy professionals, small business owners, department leads
- Budget: $500–$2,500
- Need: Practical AI workflows, tool adoption, time savings
- Decision cycle: Fast (days to weeks)
- Pain point: Overwhelmed by AI options; want to move fast without strategic waste

**Tier 2: Organizations & Leadership Teams**
- Mid-market companies, departments within larger orgs, growth-stage startups
- Budget: $12,000+
- Need: Strategic assessment, enterprise-wide AI roadmap, risk/ROI framework
- Decision cycle: Slower (weeks to months)
- Pain point: Rushing into AI without strategy; worried about ROI and integration

### Page Goals
1. Position AWC as the trusted partner for AI adoption at any scale
2. Remove friction for smaller prospects (offer them an entry point)
3. Reinforce credibility with enterprise stats and proven methodology
4. Drive qualified leads to both offerings
5. Establish the coaching tier as a foundation/feeder for potential assessment upgrades

## 11. FAQ SECTION (Recommended)

**Q: What if I'm not sure whether to do coaching or assessment?**
A: Great question. Start by answering this: Are you solving a specific workflow problem (one person
or team)? If yes, coaching. Is the problem bigger—multiple teams, unclear where AI fits, need a
formal strategy? If yes, assessment. When in doubt, book a free call and we'll help you decide.

**Q: Can I do coaching first and then upgrade to assessment?**
A: Absolutely. Many clients do. Coaching gives you hands-on experience and quick wins. Assessment
builds on that foundation to create an organizational strategy.

**Q: What if assessment doesn't feel right for us?**
A: Then we'll tell you. We've passed on assessments before when clients would be better served
by ongoing coaching or a different approach entirely. Our goal is to solve your problem, not
maximize our services.

**Q: How long does implementation take after coaching/assessment?**
A: Depends on complexity. Small workflow changes? 2–4 weeks. Larger organizational shifts?
3–6 months. We'll give you a timeline in your assessment or coaching playbook.

**Q: Do you help with implementation, or just strategy?**
A: We deliver strategy and playbooks. If you want hands-on implementation support, we offer
coaching (for smaller initiatives) or can recommend trusted partners for larger builds.

**Q: What's your timeline to get started?**
A: Coaching: typically within 1–2 weeks. Assessment: typically within 3–4 weeks. Both depend
on your availability and team bandwidth.

## 13. COPY STYLE GUIDE FOR IMPLEMENTATION

**Tone:**
- Direct. No jargon. No hype.
- Professional but approachable.
- Confident but not arrogant.
- Process-driven, outcome-focused.

**Avoid:**
- "Cutting-edge," "innovative," "game-changing"
- Passive voice
- Buzzwords: "leverage," "synergy," "unlock," "empower"
- Making promises we can't keep

**Embrace:**
- Specificity (700+ implementations, 6 weeks, $12K, 5–10 hours saved)
- Honest tradeoffs (what works, what doesn't, when to say no)
- Process language ("audit," "framework," "strategy," "implement")
- Outcome language ("saves time," "drives ROI," "sticks," "scales")

## 15. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Design / Layout Considerations:
1. **Hero Section:** Prominent, clear. Make both offerings visible (not buried).
2. **Two Offerings:** Side-by-side comparison or sequential. Recommend side-by-side
   for easy decision-making.
3. **Coaching Section:** Warm, approachable. This is entry-level. Don't make it feel
   heavy or corporate.
4. **Assessment Section:** More formal, complete. This is the serious engagement.
5. **Bridge Section:** Critical. Reduce friction for people who aren't sure.
6. **CTAs:** Two different buttons (or very clear CTAs) for each offering.
   Color-code if possible (e.g., green for coaching, blue for assessment).

### Form Strategy:
- **Coaching:** Quick form (name, email, brief description of problem)
- **Assessment:** Slightly longer form (title, company, team size, key challenge)
- Both should result in a calendar booking, not just a "we'll call you" message.

### Metrics to Track:
- Clicks to coaching CTA vs. assessment CTA
- Form completion rate for each offering
- Time to scheduling (coaching vs. assessment)
- Which audience segment converts to which offering
- Post-scheduling: Which prospects actually show up? Which close?
