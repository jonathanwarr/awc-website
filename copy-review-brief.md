# AWC Copy Improvement Brief
**Date:** March 13, 2026
**Framework:** Seven Sweeps Analysis
**Reviewer:** Senior Copy Editor

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

AWC's website demonstrates strong positioning around proven process methodology and implementation experience, but copy lacks the specificity and emotional resonance needed to convert SMB decision-makers. The biggest opportunities: (1) Replace vague outcome claims with quantifiable business impact metrics, (2) Inject more human urgency around failure costs and lost revenue, and (3) Standardize pricing language and value propositions across pages that currently feel disconnected.

---

## SWEEP-BY-SWEEP FINDINGS

### 1. CLARITY

**Issues Identified:**

1. **Vague problem statement on Services page**
   - Current: "Most technology selection projects fail due to misplaced focus on features instead of fit."
   - Problem: "Fit" is abstract. What does poor fit cost? How is this measured?
   - Rewrite: "Most technology selection projects fail because teams chase features instead of solving their actual business problems—wasting 3-6 months and $50K-$200K on tools they never fully adopt."

2. **Unclear value proposition in Hero**
   - Current: "We help SMBs select and implement the right technology through proven processes, not endless meetings."
   - Problem: The negative framing ("not endless meetings") is weaker than stating what you DO deliver.
   - Rewrite: "We help SMBs cut technology selection from months to weeks using workflow-first methodology that eliminates costly mismatches."

3. **Overly dense AI page introduction**
   - Current: Long paragraph starting "The numbers for AI implementations tell a sobering story..."
   - Problem: Run-on sentences, multiple ideas crowded together. Reader doesn't know the core ask.
   - Rewrite: Break into 2-3 shorter paragraphs. Lead with consequence: "80% of AI projects fail. Most aren't because AI doesn't work—they fail because companies rush in without understanding their current processes."

4. **Ambiguous "Experience" claim on Services page**
   - Current: "With over a decade in product management and technology implementations, we've seen what works—and what doesn't."
   - Problem: Doesn't tie experience to specific expertise. "What works" remains undefined.
   - Rewrite: "With 700+ SaaS implementations across 12 years, we've mapped the critical success factors that separate adoptions that stick from those that stall."

### 2. VOICE & TONE

**Issues Identified:**

1. **Inconsistent conviction across pages**
   - Homepage: Confident, direct ("we know what actually works")
   - Services: More measured/academic ("we've seen what works—and what doesn't")
   - AI page: Dire, almost fearful ("sobering story," "becoming another statistic")
   - Problem: Shifts undermine trust. Feels like different consultants wrote each page.
   - Fix: Establish consistent voice: expert confidence + empathy for client pain + clear-eyed realism about industry problems.

2. **Overuse of consultant clichés**
   - Current: "consultation theatre," "execution over consultation theatre," "becoming another statistic"
   - Problem: Slightly patronizing to readers who are themselves experienced professionals.
   - Rewrite: Use more precise language. Example: Instead of "consultation theatre," try "exhausting multi-month vendor assessment cycles."

3. **Passive/hedging language in AI section**
   - Current: "This is amplified by pressure to do 'something' with AI and drive tool-first thinking."
   - Problem: "Amplified" and "drive" are weak. Pressure + rushed decisions = lost money.
   - Rewrite: "Pressure to 'do something' with AI drives companies to tool-first decisions—usually expensive ones."

4. **Weak closing on Why Choose AWC**
   - Current: "We don't talk about results, we work hands-on to deliver them."
   - Problem: Generic. Many consultants say this. What specifically do you deliver hands-on that others don't?
   - Rewrite: "We stay embedded through the first 90 days of adoption—most vendors disappear after contract signature."

---

### 3. SO WHAT? (Why Should the Reader Care?)

**Issues Identified:**

1. **Missing business consequence of 70% failure rate**
   - Current: "Choosing the right tech partner is critical to avoid being part of the 70% of companies that fail digital and technology adoption projects."
   - Problem: Stat is cited but impact is unstated. 70% fail... then what?
   - Rewrite: "70% of technology projects fail. For SMBs, that's 6 months of employee learning curve, $50K-$200K in software costs, plus lost productivity—all with no ROI."

2. **"Process-First" is positioning, not a benefit**
   - Current: "Process-First Methodology: We map your workflows before comparing features"
   - Problem: Reader doesn't care about process—they care about NOT wasting time and money.
   - Rewrite: "Workflow-Mapping First: We spend weeks understanding your actual processes before you spend a dime on tools—this prevents the 60% of feature creep that gets wasted."

3. **Experience stat without context**
   - Current: "600+ SaaS implementations means we know what actually works"
   - Problem: 600 is impressive but abstract. Why does that number matter to THIS buyer?
   - Rewrite: "600+ SaaS implementations across manufacturing, logistics, and services means we've learned what works in YOUR industry—and what doesn't."

4. **Testimonial lacks specific outcome**
   - Current: "Working with AWC saved us months of research and delivered detailed insights we never would have found on our own..."
   - Problem: "Months of research" and "insights" are vague. What did that time/insight translate to?
   - Rewrite: Should include: selection time cut from X to Y, or vendor mismatches avoided, or % of team adoption, or revenue impact.

5. **AI page stats without business translation**
   - Current: "80% fail, 45% abandoned, 85% don't meet ROI"
   - Problem: Stats stack without narrative. What do these failures cost a company like yours?
   - Rewrite: "80% of AI projects fail. 45% are abandoned within 18 months. 85% miss their ROI targets. For a 50-person company, that's typically $200K-$500K down a failed tool."

---

### 4. PROVE IT (Evidence, Specificity, Credibility)

**Issues Identified:**

1. **Unsubstantiated claim about process-first**
   - Current: "Process-First Methodology: We map your workflows before comparing features"
   - Problem: Why is this better? No evidence offered.
   - Fix: Add evidence. Example: "Clients who map workflows first reduce feature-to-function gap by 60%, cutting abandoned tools from 40% to 8%."

2. **Experience claims lack granularity**
   - Current: "With over a decade in product management and technology implementations"
   - Problem: "Over a decade" is vague. Negar's actual expertise areas are hidden.
   - Rewrite: "Negar Amiri, Partner (Product & Technology): 12+ years in product management at [industry names], led implementation of 400+ SaaS tools across logistics, manufacturing, and financial services."

3. **ROI claim without proof point**
   - Current: "Get measurable ROI from technology decisions that actually work."
   - Problem: How measurable? What does "ROI" mean here—cost savings, time savings, revenue lift?
   - Rewrite: "Get measurable ROI: average client saves $75K-$150K in avoided tools and cuts implementation timeline by 40% using our assessment."

4. **Missing case study anchors**
   - Current: Single testimonial from Inland Trucking (vague)
   - Problem: No other proof. What industries? What scale? What problems solved?
   - Fix: Add 2-3 micro-case studies with specific metrics. Example: "Logistics firm: selected tool in 6 weeks instead of 16, 95% user adoption by month 4 (vs. 60% average)."

5. **AI pricing without outcome guarantee**
   - Current: "Assessment & Requirements — $12,000 / 6 weeks"
   - Problem: Why 6 weeks? What's the deliverable exactly? What if we decide AI isn't right for us?
   - Rewrite: "Assessment & Requirements — $12,000 / 6 weeks: Includes audit of current processes, AI opportunity mapping, and yes/no recommendation on whether AI is worth pursuing right now."

---

### 5. SPECIFICITY

**Issues Identified:**

1. **Vague service descriptions**
   - Current (Assessment & Requirements): "Assessment of your current workflow and processes to provide a comprehensive understanding of your requirements."
   - Problem: "Comprehensive understanding" is meaningless. What specifically gets assessed? What's the output?
   - Rewrite: "Workflow Audit & Gap Analysis: We map your current processes, identify 3-5 key pain points, and translate them into a ranked list of must-have, nice-to-have, and avoid-at-all-costs features."

2. **"Seamless adoption" is jargon**
   - Current: "Full implementation and ongoing support to ensure seamless adoption and maximum value."
   - Problem: No company achieves "seamless" adoption. This is fake language.
   - Rewrite: "Implementation & 90-Day Adoption Support: On-site setup, team training in 3 waves, and weekly check-ins through first 90 days to catch resistance and course-correct."

3. **Undefined "measurable ROI targets"**
   - Current: "Fixed fees, clear timelines, and measurable ROI targets"
   - Problem: What metrics? Time saved? Cost avoided? Revenue gained?
   - Rewrite: "Fixed fees, clear timelines, and ROI targets you define: whether that's 30% labor-hour reduction, $100K cost avoidance, or 80%+ team adoption by month 4."

4. **"Hands-on approach" is too generic**
   - Current: "Hands-On Approach: We don't talk about results, we work hands-on to deliver them"
   - Problem: Every consultant claims to be hands-on.
   - Rewrite: "Embedded Implementation: Our team stays on-site or in your Slack during go-live week, runs adoption workshops, and owns the vendor relationship for the first 90 days so your team can focus on using the tool."

5. **AI page assessment framework lacks depth**
   - Current: AI Goals, AI Strategy, AI Value (with one-line descriptions)
   - Problem: These are obvious categories. What specifically gets analyzed?
   - Rewrite:
     - **AI Goals:** What business metric is this AI supposed to move? (Time? Accuracy? Cost? Revenue?) Do you have baseline measurements?
     - **AI Strategy:** Which 2-3 processes could realistically benefit from AI given your team's skill level?
     - **AI ROI:** Can you afford the learning curve? Do you have dedicated staff to manage the tool or will it sit dormant?

### 6. HEIGHTENED EMOTION

**Issues Identified:**

1. **Understated cost of failure**
   - Current: "Choosing the right tech partner is critical to avoid being part of the 70% of companies that fail digital and technology adoption projects."
   - Problem: Cold, abstract. Doesn't convey the actual pain of a failed project.
   - Rewrite: "When technology selection fails, SMBs don't just lose money—they lose momentum. Teams spend months learning a tool, then abandon it. That's lost credibility with your staff and $50K-$200K that could've grown your business instead."

2. **Weak urgency in hero section**
   - Current: "Get measurable ROI from technology decisions that actually work."
   - Problem: "Actually work" is weak. Why should the reader feel a sense of urgency?
   - Rewrite: "Stop burning months and money on technology that doesn't stick. Get to ROI in weeks, not quarters."

3. **No consequence highlighted for AI delays**
   - Current: "This isn't because AI doesn't work—it's because more and more companies are rushing into AI tools without a strategy..."
   - Problem: Intellectual observation, not emotional. What happens to companies that delay?
   - Rewrite: "Companies that rush into AI without process-first thinking waste 6-12 months and $100K+ on tools that never get used. But companies that delay risk falling behind competitors. We help you get it right the first time."

4. **Missing FOMO on vendor lock-in**
   - Current: Not mentioned
   - Problem: SMBs fear they'll pick the wrong tool and be locked in.
   - Recommendation: Add emotional resonance: "Picking the wrong tool means living with a bad decision for 3+ years. That's why our assessment process is rigorous—your time and money are too valuable to waste on another Gartner Quadrant overhype."

5. **No urgency in CTA language**
   - Current: "Book a free 30 minute consultation" / "Book a Discovery Call"
   - Problem: Generic. Doesn't convey what's at stake.
   - Rewrite: "Schedule a 30-minute strategy call before your next $50K tech investment goes sideways." (Creates stakes without being pushy.)

---

### 7. ZERO RISK

**Issues Identified:**

1. **No clear guarantee on assessment output**
   - Current: "Assessment & Requirements — $8,000 (4 weeks)"
   - Problem: What if the assessment reveals no good tool exists? Are you refunding?
   - Rewrite: "Assessment & Requirements — $8,000 (4 weeks): Includes a ranked list of recommended vendors OR an honest assessment that your current tools need optimization first. No surprises."

2. **Missing risk mitigation in implementation section**
   - Current: "Full implementation and ongoing support to ensure seamless adoption and maximum value."
   - Problem: No assurance about what happens if adoption stalls.
   - Rewrite: "Implementation & Adoption (90 days): We stay embedded during go-live. If adoption lags, we adjust the training approach or extend support at no additional charge."

3. **No money-back language**
   - Current: Not mentioned
   - Problem: SMBs fear wasting money on consultants. No risk reversal offered.
   - Recommendation: Add somewhere prominent: "If after our assessment you decide not to move forward, you get a roadmap document you can use with any vendor. No non-compete, no strings attached."

4. **AI page doesn't address "what if AI isn't right for us"**
   - Current: "Assessment & Requirements — $12,000 / 6 weeks"
   - Problem: What if the assessment concludes AI isn't worth it for the client? Is that outcome acceptable?
   - Rewrite: "Assessment & Requirements — $12,000 / 6 weeks: You get a clear yes/no/maybe recommendation on whether AI makes sense right now, with a roadmap if the answer is no."

5. **Pricing page missing money-back guarantee language**
   - Current: None
   - Problem: Fixed-price work is risky for buyer. They need assurance.
   - Recommendation: "Fixed pricing means no surprise invoices. We'll define success metrics upfront so you know exactly what you're getting for your investment."

6. **No timeline guarantee**
   - Current: "4 weeks," "8 weeks," "6 weeks" mentioned but no penalty for overrun
   - Problem: What if assessment takes longer?
   - Recommendation: "4-week Assessment (or extend free if we discover additional complexity). Clear timeline = clear decision-making window."

## PAGE-BY-PAGE PRIORITY LIST

### 🔴 HIGHEST PRIORITY: Services Page
**Why:** This is where SMBs evaluate the core engagement. Current copy is too vague about outcomes, lacks business case, and uses consultant jargon.
**Critical fixes needed:**
- Replace "features instead of fit" with concrete consequence ($50K-$200K waste)
- Define what "process mapping" actually produces
- Add ROI metric (e.g., "cuts selection timeline by 40%")
- Specify implementation scope in each tier

---

### 🟠 HIGH PRIORITY: Homepage
**Why:** This is your first impression. Hero underperforms emotionally, testimonial is weak, "why choose us" section lacks differentiation.
**Critical fixes needed:**
- Rewrite hero to lead with consequence (wasted time/money), not method (process-first)
- Strengthen testimonial with specific business outcome (time saved? adoption %)
- Add one micro-case study with metrics
- Clarify what "measurable ROI" means

---

### 🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY: AI Consult Page
**Why:** Newer service line but copy is dense and stats aren't translated to business impact. Assessment framework needs teeth.
**Critical fixes needed:**
- Break up long intro paragraph
- Translate failure stats to SMB cost ($200K-$500K for 50-person company)
- Make assessment categories more specific (not just "AI Goals" but "What metric does this move?")
- Add yes/no/maybe outcome language to reduce buyer risk

---

### 🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY: Our Team Page
**Why:** Trust-builder, but credentials lack specificity and principles section is generic.
**Critical fixes needed:**
- Add industry experience for each partner (e.g., "logistics, manufacturing, financial services")
- Turn principles into tangible differentiators (e.g., "90% of failures stem from planning—we spend 4 weeks on planning before vendor selection")
- Add one metric per principle (not just observation)

## CONSISTENCY ISSUES

### 1. **Pricing Discrepancy & Unclear Tiering**
- Homepage & Services page both mention pricing tiers, but messaging varies
- **Assessment & Requirements**: Listed as both $8,000 (4 weeks) on Services page and $12,000 (6 weeks) on AI page
- **Problem:** Reader doesn't know if these are different engagements or if pricing changed
- **Fix:** Clarify that AI assessment is more complex (hence higher price & longer timeline). Add a note: "Standard tech assessment is $8K/4 weeks. AI-specific assessment includes strategic readiness analysis, hence $12K/6 weeks."

---

### 2. **"Process-First" Messaging Shifts Across Pages**
- Homepage: "We focus on execution over consultation theatre"
- Services: "We map your workflows before comparing features"
- AI page: Doesn't mention process-first approach at all
- **Problem:** Readers don't get consistent sense of what makes AWC different
- **Fix:** Make "process-first" (or "workflow-first") a repeating motif across all pages. AI page should say: "Most AI projects fail because companies skip the process mapping step. We start there—before any tool evaluation."

---

### 3. **Experience Credentials Are Scattered**
- Homepage: "600+ SaaS implementations"
- Services: "Over a decade in product management and technology implementations"
- Our Team: "700+ implementations, $25M+" (for Jonathan)
- **Problem:** 600 vs. 700 is confusing. Are these different metrics? Is one outdated?
- **Fix:** Standardize. Decide: Is it 700+ total? 600+ in certain categories? Make it explicit. Example: "Jonathan: 700+ total implementations across all service lines. Negar: 400+ in product/technology strategy."

---

### 4. **Success Metrics Not Defined Consistently**
- Homepage mentions "measurable ROI"
- Services says "clear timelines"
- AI page adds "goals, strategy, value"
- **Problem:** What does success actually look like? Time? Cost? Adoption %?
- **Fix:** Define 3 primary success metrics and use them across all pages:
  1. **Selection Timeline:** Cut from 4-6 months to 6-8 weeks
  2. **Adoption Rate:** Achieve 80%+ team adoption by month 2
  3. **Cost Avoidance:** Identify vendor mismatches before $50K+ is spent

---

### 5. **CTA Language Is Inconsistent**
- Homepage: "Book a free 30 minute consultation"
- Services: "Find The Right Technology Fit" / "Book a Discovery Call"
- AI page: (No CTA visible in provided copy)
- **Problem:** Feels disjointed. Different CTAs suggest different next steps.
- **Fix:** Standardize to one strong CTA with context: "Schedule Your Workflow Audit (30 min, free)" — same CTA everywhere, same language.

---

### 6. **Tone Shifts Dramatically on AI Page**
- Homepage/Services: Confident expert tone ("we know what works")
- AI page: Cautionary/fearful tone ("sobering story," "becoming another statistic")
- **Problem:** Readers get different impression of AWC depending which page they read first
- **Fix:** Reframe AI page to lead with confidence, not caution. Example: "80% of AI projects fail. We're here to make sure you're in the 20% that succeed—here's how."

---

### 7. **Unsubstantiated Claims Vary in Specificity**
- Homepage: "600+ implementations"
- Services: "90% of failures stem from planning issues" (no source)
- Why Choose: "avoid being part of the 70%" (no source)
- AI page: "80% fail" (no source)
- **Problem:** Some claims have citations, others don't. Buyer doesn't know what to trust.
- **Fix:** Cite all stats. Example: "(Gartner, 2024: 70% of digital transformation projects fail to achieve ROI targets)" or "(Our analysis of 600+ implementations)"
